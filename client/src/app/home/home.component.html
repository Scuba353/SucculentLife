Soil

The best soil for succulents in pots needs to drain quickly, to prevent root rot, yet hold water long enough for the roots to absorb it. There are a number of commercialy available mixes specific to cacti and succulents like the one in the photo.

Light and Water

Succulents indoors need as much light as possible. They should be placed by a window that gets light a majority of the day! They dont need as much water though. Generally, once a week is enough. Throughly wet the soil and allow it to dry before rewatering.

A Leaf

Lastly, you need the plants. One leaf with a good break from the parent is sufficient but to increase succuess you can start 3-4 leaves. Worst case is you end up wiht that many mature pants!

About. Succulent Life!

My succulent addiction began with a number of single leaf cuttings from my mother who has a ton of space and a ton of succulents. From these leaves I have grown a full collection of mature plants and am always looking for new species that I don't already have. This website is dedicated to teaching you more about growing your own succulents. Why would you want to pay money for individual plants from the store when you can grow them on your own in just a couple easy steps? Propogation is a method of growing plants from a small cutting of the parent plant, a single leaf in the case of most succulents! Check out the propogation section for some additional information on this growing method. If you are new to the succulent life and would like to request some cuttings lets get started. Find some friends through this site and request a few leaves. In a matter of time you'll have a number of different species and be on your way to having plants large enough to share with others!
